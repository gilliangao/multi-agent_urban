# Portfolio Summary: Emergent Canopy

**Emergent Canopy** is an explainable multi-agent platform for urban climate planning. It helps cities identify where to plant trees by combining geospatial evidence, specialist AI agents, agent debate, visual analytics, and auditable recommendation traces.

## Why It Matters

Urban climate decisions involve trade-offs: cooling, pollution, equity, biodiversity, feasibility, and cost. This project shows how AI agents can represent those competing priorities, debate the evidence, and help planners understand why a recommendation is made.

## What It Demonstrates

- Real geospatial data processing with trees, roads, parks, buildings, and boundaries.
- Multi-objective scoring for candidate planting sites.
- Specialist agents for heat, pollution, equity, biodiversity, feasibility, critique, and judgement.
- Explainable AI outputs through vote heatmaps, debate transcripts, reason chains, and trace JSON.
- A working Streamlit interface with maps, charts, controls, and planning outputs.

## Best Demo Story

1. Select an area.
2. Generate candidate planting cells.
3. Show the map of recommended sites.
4. Compare sites across climate and feasibility indicators.
5. Show how each agent voted.
6. Show the deliberation transcript and judge summary.
7. Export or inspect the trace for auditability.
